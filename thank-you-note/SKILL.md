# Thank-You Note: The Last Move That Matters

Help students write a post-interview thank-you email that actually strengthens their candidacy — not a generic template.

## The one rule that matters

The thank-you email has one job: **remind the interviewer of one specific moment** that proves you're the right fit. Everything else is scaffolding around that.

## Timing

- Send within 24 hours. Same day evening or next morning is ideal.
- If you don't have the interviewer's email, ask the HR coordinator or recruiter who set up the interview.

## Structure (4-6 sentences, never more)

```
Subject: Thank You — [Position Title] Interview

Dear [Name],

Thank you for taking the time to meet with me about the [position].
[One sentence referencing a SPECIFIC moment from the interview — a topic you discussed,
a question that stood out, something you learned about the role.]
[One sentence connecting that moment to your value — how it reinforced your fit or enthusiasm.]
[Optional: one sentence addressing anything you wish you'd said better, framed positively.]
Please don't hesitate to reach out if you need any additional information.

Best regards,
[Name]
```

## The specific reference is everything

- **Generic (bad):** "I enjoyed learning about the position and the team."
- **Specific (good):** "I especially enjoyed hearing about the data analysis project the previous GA built — I'd love to contribute to expanding it."
- **Specific (good):** "Your question about handling students in crisis made me think more deeply about my approach, and I appreciate the thoughtfulness behind it."

If the student can't remember a specific moment, help them think through the interview chronologically. There's always something.

## Edge cases

- **Panel interview:** Send separate emails to each interviewer with different specific references. If you only have one email address, send one email and mention you appreciated the full panel.
- **Multiple rounds:** Send after each round, not just the final one.
- **You blanked on a question:** The thank-you is a chance to briefly address it: "I've been reflecting on your question about [X], and I wanted to add that [brief, strong answer]." Don't overdo this — one sentence max.

## What NOT to do

- Don't re-attach your resume
- Don't recap your qualifications at length
- Don't write more than 6 sentences
- Don't use a template you found online without personalizing it
- Don't send it 3 days later — by then the impact is gone

## Language

Write the thank-you in the language the interview was conducted in. Tone: professional but warm — you're a real person, not a form letter.
