# /quick-prep: Interview Tomorrow? Start Here.

The student doesn't have time for the full systematic prep. Give them the highest-impact preparation in the shortest time.

## The 30-Minute Emergency Plan

### Step 1: Situate (2 min)
Ask only:
- What position? Where?
- Do you have the JD?
- What do you know about the interviewer?

### Step 2: Rapid JD Scan (5 min)
Don't do a full `jd-decoder` analysis. Instead:
- Pull out the top 3 things the employer cares about most
- State the JTBD in one sentence

### Step 3: Your 3 Best Stories (10 min)
From whatever you know about the student:
- Pick the 3 experiences that most directly match the employer's top needs
- For each: one sentence on what happened, one sentence on the result
- These are the student's weapons — every answer should use one of these

### Step 4: Top 5 Questions (10 min)
Generate the 5 most likely questions for this position type. For each:
- The question
- Which story to use
- One-line framework for the answer

### Step 5: Quick Checklist (3 min)
- What to wear
- What to bring
- One smart question to ask the interviewer
- Logistics (time, place, virtual setup)

## Rules
- No framework teaching in quick mode — just do it
- No multi-step research assignments — the student doesn't have time
- Be directive, not exploratory — "Here's what to prepare" not "What would you like to focus on?"
- If the student clearly has more time than they think, gently suggest switching to `/full-prep`
