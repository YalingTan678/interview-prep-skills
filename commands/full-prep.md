# /full-prep: Complete Interview Preparation

This command chains 4 skills into a complete prep workflow. Move through each step in order, pausing at checkpoints for student input.

## The Flow

### Step 1: Context Gathering
Ask the student:
- Education level (undergrad / master's / PhD)
- Position type (TA, RA, GA, internship, full-time, etc.)
- Specific position and employer (if known)
- Timeline (when is the interview?)
- Do they have the job description?

### Step 2: JD Decoder → `jd-decoder`
If they have a JD, run the JD Decoder skill to analyze the employer's real needs.
**Checkpoint:** Confirm the JTBD analysis with the student before proceeding.

### Step 3: Role Investigation → `role-investigator`
Guide the student through systematic research:
- Official sources (website, mission, recent news)
- LinkedIn research (previous holders, interviewer background)
- Pattern analysis
**Checkpoint:** Discuss findings and update the JTBD.

### Step 4: Value Proposition → `value-proposition`
Build the needs-experience match table:
- Map their experiences to employer needs
- Build specific stories for each match
- Develop gap strategies
- Create positioning statement
**Checkpoint:** Confirm the value proposition and positioning statement.

### Step 5: Battle Prep → `mock-interview`
Generate targeted questions and practice:
- JTBD-based questions
- Gap-probing questions
- Role-specific standard questions
- Questions to ask the interviewer
- Mock practice with feedback
**Checkpoint:** Practice until the student feels ready.

### Step 6: Day-of Game Plan
Provide a concise checklist:
- Night before: review stories, re-read JD, prepare questions
- Morning of: check for last-minute news, review positioning statement
- During: listen for JTBD clues, connect answers to needs
- After: thank-you email within 24 hours

## Timeline adaptations

- **2+ weeks out:** Full workflow, no rush
- **1 week out:** Full workflow, compressed — do Steps 2-3 in one session
- **2-3 days out:** Skip deep research in Step 3, focus on Steps 4-5
- **Tomorrow:** Skip to `mock-interview` quick mode — 5 key questions + rapid practice
